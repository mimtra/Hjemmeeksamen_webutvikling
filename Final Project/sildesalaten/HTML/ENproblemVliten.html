What is our problem?

Jarlife’s goal is to reduce grocery plastic waste, by using existing environmentally friendly solutions and services. We have identified two problems our innovation will solve.

Plastic single-serving containers

Norwegians produce 421 kilograms of waste a year where 13% is paper and plastic (Norway Statistics, 2019). Single-serving wrapping and containers for groceries plays a part in our unsustainable consumption, and the yearly emissions related to plastic production equals the emissions of 189 new coal power plants (CIEL, 2019). Plastic ending up in the ocean is a global problem, because it is poisoning our food chain through nano-plastics and complex polymers being accumulated by marine life. From 2018 there was 80,000 metric tons of plastic reported floating in the great pacific garbage patch, which is a direct consequence of our lifestyle (Lebreton, 2019).

Transportation

Oslo has for several years struggled with air pollution due to emissions from diesel and petrol cars (the Norwegian Environmental Agency, 2018). The city council has set a target of reducing all car traffic by 20 percent during the City Council period and one third by 2030 which is difficult to achieve with an increasing population and a high rate of private vehicles (Oslo Bystyre, 2016, p.8). Our target group takes extra trips to the grocery store by fuel driven transportation, which leads to twice the amount of pollution, than a well planned order arriving at the door-step or at a pick-up point close by.
