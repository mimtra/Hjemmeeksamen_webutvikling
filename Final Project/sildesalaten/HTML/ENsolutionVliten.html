FOUNDERS OF JARLIFE

What is our solution?

In recent years, the market for companies that provide online supermarkets combined with door delivery has expanded rapidly. Companies like Kolonial and Adams Matkasse have become well known brands with an increasing amount of users. The way groceries are purchased and delivered is changing, but we believe that a change is also needed in how food is packaged. 37% of groceries are wrapped in plastic (Naturvernforbundet, 2019). We wish to contribute to decrease that percentage and provide an online supermarket based on one principle; sustainable delivery and packaging of food. We believe that customers care about the environmental impact of the products they purchase and are willing to change habits if the solution is good enough.

How will we do it?

Our solution are divided into three parts to provide a more sustainable way to purchase food.

1. Online supermarket:

Our online supermarket will provide the customer with a variety of products to purchase, along with information about how and where the product is produced. To minimize the risk of food going to waste the store will suggest quantities of a specific product if the user specifies how many people he/she is ordering for.

2. Alternatives to plastic:

To decrease the amount of plastic used in wrapping products we will use alternative materials. Examples of this:

Glass containers for products that can be kept dry, for example nuts, grains and selected vegetables.


Linen bags for delivery of vegetables and bread.


Wax can be used on several products, for example cheese. Easy to clean and re-use.

Food packed in reuseable linen bags. Photo: Evgeny Glazunov.

3. Delivery:

On delivery the customer will be able to return the linen bags and glass jars from the last delivery. This enables us to re-use a lot of the containers and handle the disposal of broken jars and bags through sustainable methods. The customer will be able to choose from two different delivery options, door delivery or pick-up point. Door delivery will be made with electric cars to keep emissions as low as possible. For pick-up points we wish to collaborate with already existing supermarket, and the customer will get a discount on the total amount if pick-up point is chosen. Empty glass jars and linen bags can be returned when picking up the new shipment.

By replacing plastic packaging with other materials we want to show the industry that it is possible to change consumer and production habits. In the solution we are promoting the option to collect their delivery at a pick-up point. If more orders will be sent to a pick-up point, this would decrease the emissions incurred by delivering to individual households.
